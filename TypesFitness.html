Types of Fitness

Fitness is often referred to as physical fitness. But there are many Types of Fitness and each can be trained in their own time. A gentle reminder that fitness is not all about being physical, and it includes nutrition fitness, mental, emotional fitnesses too, but for this we will focus on the common types of Fitness.


Cardiovascular Fitness

Cardiovascular, or commonly known as Cardio, is our body's ability to deal with oxygen. It's a type of exercise that trains our lungs and heart to efficiently distribute oxygen throughout the body and use those oxygen to fuel our movements.

Common Activities Are:

  1. Walking
  2. Brisk Walking - Faster Paced Walks
  3. Jogging
  4. Running
  5. Jump Rope
  6. Stair Climbing
  7. Jumping Jacks

Muscular Strength

This is commonly known as the amount of force a muscle can exert, or even commonly known as how strong you are. There are my types of muscle strength such as; Agile Strength, Speed Strength, Explosive Strength, etc.

Common Activities Are:

  1. Pushups
  2. Deadlifts
  3. Lunge
  4. Squats
  5. Planks
  6. Bicep Curls
  7. Pullips

Muscular Endurance

This type of fitness means the amount of times our muscles can work without getting tired. Endurance let's use do more activities for longer periods of time. This go hand-in-hand with Muscular Srength, and it's hard to have one without the other.

Common Activities Are:

  1. Imaginary Chair - Sitting against the Wall Without a Chair
  2. Calisthenics
  3. Lunge
  4. Squats
  5. Planks
  6. Bicep Curls
  7. Pullips

The exercises do not stray that far from Muscular Strength exercises!

Flexibility

Flexibility is our muscle's ability to stretch and lengthen. It is about moving our joints smoothly without feeling any stiffness from our muscles. The most common type of exercise can be Yoga, and Yoga is known to also test your muscular, cardio, and endurance so it can be good to pair flexiblity exercises with the other types of fitness.
